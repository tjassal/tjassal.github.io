Research focused in biogeography, spatial ecology
and landscape change

Our goal is to provide sound science to both resource managers and policy makers to help shape ecosystem management and conservation as we move into an uncertain future.

We are still in the process of getting the site set up. In the meantime please see timassal.com for more info.

    The lab is housed in the Department of Geography at Kent State University. We frame broad questions related to ecosystem resilience, resistance and vulnerability with regard to natural and anthropogenic disturbance. Focal points include:

    • investigation of disturbance events
    • status and trends of key natural resources
    • assessment of management actions on the conservation of populations, communities, and ecosystems

    All three of these elements are critical to gain an understanding of future scenarios related to increased pressures on natural resources, most notably through climate change and human population growth.
